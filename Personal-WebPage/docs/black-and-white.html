Black and White

C# | Unity | Maya | Technical Art Pipeline

View GitHub Repository

The Vision: Order vs. Chaos

Black and White was conceived as a dark fantasy action game built around a "one body, two souls" core mechanic. The narrative follows Aeon, a guardian whose soul is violently split into two opposing entities: Black, representing raw chaos, rebellion, and primal instinct, and White, representing absolute, unyielding order and rationality. The gameplay was designed to reflect this dichotomy, forcing the player to navigate a hostile world outside the city walls while balancing two entirely different combat skill trees.

Engineering the Art Pipeline

Inspiration & Research

The Bottleneck: In a previous project, I spent over a month agonizingly hand-drawing just 100 frames of simple 3-frame attack animations. It was inefficient and creatively draining. For an action game requiring fluid, complex combat, traditional frame-by-frame pixel art was mathematically impossible for a small indie team.

The Research: I began researching alternative workflows and discovered an interview with the art director of Dead Cells. They faced the exact same problem and solved it by rendering 3D models into 2D pixel animations using a custom software pipeline. Inspired, I set out to reverse-engineer and adapt this pipeline for our Unity project.

Implementation

  • Rapid 3D Prototyping: Having no prior 3D modeling experience, I spent 10 hours learning the basics from scratch to create our first functional character mesh.
  • Automated Rigging & Animation: Instead of hand-animating, I utilized Mixamo to automatically generate rigs that perfectly preserved the model's posture. I then sourced high-quality 3D animations (attacks, dodges, blocks) from the Unity Asset Store, allowing them to share the same underlying bone structure.
  • Unity Shaders & Rendering: I adapted an open-source shader pipeline (PixelArtPipeline by Broxxar) inside Unity. By adjusting the model scale factor and forcing the animations to play in-place (stripping root X/Z transforms), I could capture the 3D movements perfectly.
  • Sprite Sheet Generation: The final step of the pipeline automatically captures the shaded 3D model at 60fps and exports it directly into Unity's 2D URP texture folders as a ready-to-use 2D sprite sheet.
